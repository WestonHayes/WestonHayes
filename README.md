Professional Bio
I have a geology degree, an MBA, and a Lean Six Sigma Black Belt — which means I can identify the exact type of rock your organization is stuck under, calculate the cost of being stuck there, and then systematically eliminate the inefficiencies keeping you stuck. I'm also a PMP and Certified Scrum Master, because apparently I collect certifications the way other people collect hobbies. I also hold certifications in wetland delineation, environmental science, and GIS — not because I still do those things, but because the certification was there and I have a problem.

My career has taken me through capital markets, treasury, and now Senior Vice President of Operational Risk and Regulatory Engagement — a title that sounds like I fight compliance dragons for a living, which is essentially accurate.

On the analytical side, I live and breathe spreadsheets — and when a spreadsheet isn't enough, I write R code, because sometimes the data deserves to suffer alongside you in a more sophisticated environment.

In my garage, I have built a valveless pulse jet engine. From there, I decided the logical next step was to make a 3-foot model rocket land itself — because I had the same dream as SpaceX but not the same budget. I designed the thrust vector controller, wrote the flight computer code on an Elegoo, and soldered the PCB myself. It lands itself. I did not have to call anyone for help. These are both victories.

I have also solved a 12-sided Rubik's Cube. A dodecahedron. The one that looks like a fever dream. I don't want to talk about how long it took, but I do want you to know that it happened.

I am, to be clear, absolutely not the smartest person in any given room. What I am is epically, almost offensively resourceful — and it turns out that gap is a lot smaller than advertised. Resourcefulness is just intelligence with better problem-solving shoes on. I've gotten surprisingly far on this strategy and have no plans to stop.

My skill set sits at the intersection of "why on earth would anyone want to know that" and "oh wow, that would be incredibly handy for bar trivia." Somehow this has translated into a career. I remain grateful and mildly suspicious.
